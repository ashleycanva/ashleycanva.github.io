The Client

A Concept Houston Had Never Seen Before

This Taiwanese restaurant brought a fresh dining concept to Houstonindividual hotpot, a format where each guest gets their own personal pot, their own flavors, and their own experience. It was one of the first of its kind in all of Texas.

Located in the heart of Houston's Chinatown, surrounded by well-established names like Sinh Sinh and House Of Bowl, the restaurant faced the double challenge of introducing an unfamiliar concept while competing in one of the most restaurant-dense neighborhoods in the city. When I came in, their Instagram had 200–300 followers and they had no Facebook page, no affiliate strategy, and no consistent content presence.

The Challenge

Selling a Concept People Had Never Tried

Breaking into a competitive market with a brand-new dining format meant the restaurant had to educate customers before it could sell to them.

An Unfamiliar Dining Concept

Individual hotpot was a new idea for most Houston diners. Before selling the experience, the restaurant had to explain what it wasand why it was worth trying over familiar alternatives.

Starting from Near Zero Online

With only 200–300 Instagram followers and no Facebook page, the restaurant had virtually no digital footprintin a market where social proof drives dining decisions.

Fierce Local Competition

Chinatown is home to some of Houston's most beloved Chinese and Taiwanese restaurants. Standing out against deeply established competitors with loyal followings required a clear, compelling brand voice.

No Marketing Infrastructure

No affiliate partnerships, no ad strategy, no content calendar, and no established visual identity. Everything needed to be built from scratchquicklyas the restaurant was already open and serving customers.

The Approach

Three Pillars of Growth

01

Paid Advertising & Social Media Growth

Took full ownership of the restaurant's Instagram and built their Facebook page from the ground up. Launched targeted ad campaigns focused on Houston-area residents most likely to be curious about a new dining experienceyoung food enthusiasts, Taiwanese/Asian food lovers, and Chinatown regulars. Added a retargeting layer to re-engage past visitors who had already shown interest. Operating on a lean $400/month budget, paid advertising alone drove 1,000+ new followers and brought a steady stream of new customers through the door.

📍 Greater Houston targeting + retargeting · $400/month budget · 1,000+ followers from paid ads
02

Affiliate Marketing & Community Building

Launched an affiliate marketing program by partnering with local food bloggers, Houston-based foodies, and community influencers who could authentically introduce the individual hotpot concept to their audiences. By offering tastings, exclusive access, and referral incentives, I built a network of advocates who generated organic buzzdriving the follower count to 3,000+ and establishing word-of-mouth in a market where peer recommendations carry significant weight.

🤝 Local food influencers · affiliate referrals · authentic community-led growth
03

Content Creation & Marketing Collateral

Developed a consistent visual identity across all social platforms through in-house food photography, video content, and designed marketing materials. Created promotional posters for new dishes and seasonal drinks, capturing the restaurant's atmosphere and the unique individual hotpot experience in a way that made the concept immediately inviting and easy to understand for first-time visitors. Every piece of content was designed to educate, excite, and convert.

📸 In-house food photography · promotional posters · consistent social visual identity
